How we work

A working method, not a methodology deck.

We've sat in enough CISO chairs to know that security work is judged by what happens during an incident or an audit — not by the binder. The point is operating capability.

Step 01

Understand what you must defend.

We start with the regulatory perimeter, the business model, and the actual data flows. The threat model follows the business — not a generic framework.

Step 02

Map control to risk to evidence.

Every control we recommend ties to a named risk and the evidence an auditor will ask for. No control exists for its own sake.

Step 03

Make the gaps fixable.

A remediation plan with owners, dates, and budget. We sequence work so the highest-risk gaps close first — and the audit-visible gaps close before the auditor arrives.

Step 04

Brief the board, in their language.

A quarterly report a non-technical audit committee can read in fifteen minutes — risks, what we're doing about them, and what we still owe.
