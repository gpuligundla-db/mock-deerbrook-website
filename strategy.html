Section 01

Brand assessment.

What's working, what's outdated, and what the firm is becoming.

Where we stand

Deer Brook is a credible mid-market technology firm with a strong Microsoft Dynamics practice and quietly capable practices in cybersecurity, higher education, data, and cloud. The market — including its own clients — perceives it as "a Microsoft shop with extra services." That perception is the problem.

Strengths

  • Eleven years of operator-grade delivery.
  • Real Dynamics depth — F&O, BC, GP migrations.
  • Niche authority in higher education.
  • Senior practitioners; low staff churn.

Weaknesses

  • Reads as a VAR / MSP from outside.
  • AI story is absent on the current site.
  • Practice areas presented as separate silos.
  • No clear executive-level narrative for CFO/CIO buyers.

What is outdated

Current site reads as a 2018 partner page — heavy on Microsoft logos, light on point of view. Lacks any sustained narrative about what Deer Brook actually believes about modern operations. Stock-feeling imagery. Practice areas listed but not differentiated. No published thinking.

What is confusing

The relationship between "Deer Brook Consulting" and "Deer Brook Dynamics" is unclear. Visitors can't tell whether AI is a service, a horizontal, or marketing language. The page doesn't make obvious that Deer Brook works outside the Microsoft ecosystem at all.

Missed opportunities

  • The AI horizontal. No competitor at this size has staked a clean position on AI as operating philosophy.
  • Higher education. Deep operating knowledge — currently buried as a sub-bullet.
  • Published thinking. Field notes from 180+ engagements is an asset; none of it is in market.
  • Vendor-agnostic positioning. A real differentiator vs. partner-driven competitors.

What Deer Brook should become

A modern, AI-forward technology consulting firm whose Microsoft Dynamics practice is one capability inside a broader operational-transformation platform — and whose published point of view earns the right to be in the consideration set for CFOs, CIOs, and CISOs who are actively skeptical of both VARs and global consultancies.

Section 02

New positioning.

One sentence, then everything else.

Positioning statement

Deer Brook is a modern technology consulting firm helping organizations modernize finance, security, data, higher-education systems, and operations through AI-enabled transformation.

AI sits above every practice as a unifying operating philosophy — not below them as a separate service line.

Tagline options

  1. Operational transformation for the AI era.
  2. Modernize operations. Native to AI.
  3. The work above the workflow.
  4. Consulting for the systems era.
  5. Built where compliance is non-negotiable.

Messaging pillars

  • AI as operating philosophy. AI is delivered through every practice, not next to them.
  • Practitioner-led. Senior operators on every engagement. No bait-and-switch staffing.
  • Vendor-agnostic, in writing. No referral fees. The right tool, not the house tool.
  • Built for regulated complexity. Higher ed, government, nonprofit, mid-market — where the wrong system costs more than no system.

Differentiators against the field

  • Vs. global consultancies (Accenture, Deloitte): Senior practitioners only; fixed-scope, milestone-billed engagements; no inflated benches.
  • Vs. Microsoft-only resellers / VARs: AI-native delivery patterns; multi-platform; published point of view.
  • Vs. boutique AI shops: Real depth in ERP, security, and higher-ed verticals; eleven years of audited operating delivery.

"Why Deer Brook"

We don't make slideware. We ship working systems — designed so intelligence is native, not retrofitted.

Practice page pattern (one template, eight instances)

Every practice page follows the same structure so the architecture scales: hero · three engagement offers · process · case · stack · FAQ · related practices · CTA. Tightly templated. The discipline lets the team publish a new sub-practice in a day.

Industry pages

Cross-cut the practices. A Higher Education industry page pulls SIS work from Higher-Ed Practice, student-data from Data & Analytics, GLBA from Cybersecurity, and AI policy from AI & Automation — proof that practices compose.

Conversion strategy

  • Single canonical CTA: "Send us a paragraph." Routes to a partner. Two-day reply commitment.
  • Secondary CTA on practice pages: "Scope a starting engagement."
  • Newsletter (The Brook) is the long-tail conversion. Captures CIO/CFO leads on read-only intent.

Section 06

SEO + GEO strategy.

Optimize for traditional search and for being cited by ChatGPT, Claude, and Google AI Overviews.

Architecture principles

  • Topic clusters, not orphan pages. Each practice page is a hub; sub-practices and field notes link inward.
  • Answer-style copy. Headings phrased as questions where natural. Direct, single-sentence answers in the first paragraph.
  • Structured data everywhere. Service, FAQ, Article, Person, and Organization schema on every applicable page.
  • Canonical FAQ hub. Aggregated answer page that AI search engines cite preferentially.

Semantic keyword clusters

AI & Automation
enterprise AI consulting · agentic workflows · AI governance for finance · mid-market AI implementation · AI usage policy template · fractional CAIO
Microsoft Dynamics
Dynamics 365 consulting partner · D365 F&O implementation · Business Central migration · Dynamics GP end-of-life · Power Platform consulting
Higher Education
higher ed AI policy · Anthology implementation · student data warehouse · GLBA compliance higher ed · SIS integration consulting
Cybersecurity
CrowdStrike implementation partner · Tenable vulnerability management · AI SOC operations · identity hardening consulting · Microsoft Defender XDR

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

AI search engines (ChatGPT search, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) cite sources that are concise, declarative, structured, and authoritative. Tactics:

  • Lead with the answer. First sentence under every H2 is the answer to the heading.
  • Numbered, bounded lists. "In 9 pages." "Four milestones." "Three engagement types." LLMs cite enumerable content.
  • Named-author bylines. Every field note signed by a partner with bio schema. Authority is a person, not a brand.
  • FAQ hub at /insights/faq/ with one canonical answer per common question — what AI search engines actually retrieve.
  • Templates and playbooks open and indexable. The "AI policy in 9 pages" becomes the citation target for every "how to write an AI policy" query.

Authority building

  • The Brook · weekly newsletter · 1,000 net-new CIO/CFO subscribers per quarter.
  • Field notes · two per practice per quarter; partners author.
  • Open playbooks · AI policy template, IT due-diligence checklist, ERP-modernization runbook.
  • Selective speaking · 8 events per year, partner-led, vertical-focused (EDUCAUSE, NACUBO, etc.).
Section 07

Visual + UX direction.

An editorial system that signals "operator-grade, modern, AI-native" without leaning on AI imagery.

Visual identity direction

Editorial · executive · operator-grade. Closer to a serious financial publication than to a SaaS marketing site. Generous whitespace. Restrained color. Typography is the primary identity element.

Typography

  • Display: Source Serif 4 — modern serif with editorial credibility. Used for h1–h3 and pull quotes.
  • Body: Geist Sans — clean geometric sans, optimized for reading. Used for body, UI, navigation.
  • System: Geist Mono — used for labels, metadata, system status. Signals "we know infrastructure."

Color palette

  • Paper: #F4F1EA — warm off-white. Background.
  • Ink: #1A1A18 — warm ink black. Primary type.
  • Forest: #2E4A3A — a deep, dignified green. Brand accent. (Nods to "Deer Brook" without being literal.)
  • Sienna: #B5532A — emergency accent only. Used sparingly.
  • Bold-mode inversion: #0E100D ground with #C5F26A electric lime — used for the campaign-feeling alternate, AI-narrative microsites, and event takeovers.

Motion principles

  • Reveal on scroll (subtle, single-axis). No parallax.
  • Slow ticker for system status, marquee for practice listing (bold mode only).
  • Cursor follows: no. Magnetic buttons: no. Whatever feels like a marketing site: no.

Imagery direction

No stock photography. No abstract AI gradient art. Two image styles only:

  • Documentary photography — actual working sessions, partner portraits, on-site delivery moments. Commissioned, B&W or warm-toned.
  • Striped placeholders + monospace labels — until real photography is shot, every image slot is a textured striped placeholder labelled in monospace. The placeholder itself is part of the brand language.

Reference companies

Aesthetic adjacents (not clones): Anthropic, Linear, McKinsey Quarterly, the New York Times opinion section, Stripe, Workday's executive site, the Financial Times. Tonally: Anthropic's confidence + McKinsey's gravity + a small operator-tools shop's directness.

Section 08

Technical build.

Built for SEO, GEO, and content velocity.

Stack recommendation

Next.js + Sanity — primary recommendation. Next.js for performance, MDX-rich field notes, and structured-data control. Sanity for partner-authored content with low friction. Hosted on Vercel.

Alternative: Webflow if the marketing team must be able to edit without dev support. Acceptable for first 18 months; revisit at scale.

Why not WordPress

WordPress is fine. It is not differentiating, and it caps the marketing team's velocity on structured data and component reuse. The cost of moving off it in three years is higher than the cost of starting on Next.js + Sanity now.

SEO stack

  • Server-rendered routes, single canonical URL per page.
  • JSON-LD schema injection per template (Service, FAQ, Article, Person, Organization).
  • Sitemap auto-generation, Robots tuned for AI crawlers (allow GPTBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended).
  • Ahrefs or Semrush for content gap analysis; Search Console + Bing Webmaster baseline.

Analytics & routing

  • Plausible (privacy-friendly default) + GA4 (legacy obligations).
  • HubSpot for lead capture and routing (CRM already in use).
  • Form posts route to partners by practice tag; two-day SLA tracked in CRM.

GEO architecture

  • FAQ hub at /insights/faq/ with canonical Q→A pairing per page.
  • Per-page llms.txt manifests (an emerging convention) summarizing the page for LLM crawlers.
  • Partner author pages with full bio schema and external citations (LinkedIn, prior writing, conference talks).
  • Open templates and playbooks served as canonical, indexable pages — not gated PDFs.
Section 09

Implementation roadmap.

Phased so the firm is visibly different in 60 days and meaningfully repositioned in 12 months.

Phase 01 · 0–60 days

Quick wins.

  • Ship new homepage (editorial direction).
  • Ship Practices index + AI & Automation page.
  • About + Contact + reply commitment.
  • Launch The Brook newsletter (week 4).
  • Tagline + positioning in market.
Phase 02 · 60–120 days

Practice depth.

  • Six remaining practice pages live.
  • Industry pages (5).
  • FAQ hub seeded with 40 entries.
  • First four field notes published.
  • Schema + GEO architecture in place.
Phase 03 · 120–240 days

Content velocity.

  • Weekly field notes cadence.
  • Open playbooks published (3).
  • Documentary photography shot.
  • Partner author pages + LinkedIn alignment.
  • First conference talks placed.
Phase 04 · 240–365 days

Authority compounding.

  • 20+ field notes, 8 playbooks.
  • Newsletter at 5,000 subscribers.
  • Cited by AI search engines on target queries.
  • Inbound at > 60% of qualified leads.
  • Phase plan for international + new verticals.

Lead-generation roadmap

  • Direct response: "Send us a paragraph" is the canonical CTA. Two-day reply SLA tracked.
  • Newsletter: The Brook — primary long-tail capture. CIO/CFO segmentation from sign-up.
  • Open playbooks: AI policy, IT due diligence, ERP modernization. Each indexed and citable.
  • Selective events: Eight per year, partner-led, vertical-focused.
  • Referral discipline: No vendor referral fees accepted. No outbound paid acquisition for first 12 months.

Success metrics

  • Inbound qualified leads quarterly · target 30 by Q4.
  • Newsletter subscribers · 5,000 by month 12.
  • AI-citation rate on target queries · 25% by month 12 (manual audit).
  • Organic search · top-3 for "Dynamics 365 consulting partner [region]" by month 9.
  • Average deal velocity from inbound · < 45 days first touch to engagement signed.
